About Me

Hi there! I am a junior-year Physics student at Birla Institute of Technology, Mesra, pursuing an Integrated M.Sc. in Physics with a minor in Mathematics and Computing. I am passionate about astronomy and cosmology, especially in exploring how theoretical models connect with observational data. My interests range from galaxies, black holes, and gravitational waves to stellar evolution and the large-scale structure of the universe.

I enjoy working in astronomy and cosmology not just for the breathtaking images, but because it is challenging. It constantly pushes the limits of my intellect, occasionally bruises my ego, and reminds me that no matter how much I know, the universe always knows more. Sometimes I work until four in the morning, fueled by curiosity, fascination, and an unshakable stubbornness to understand something new. Other times I show up to class sleep-deprived, with a cup of coffee in hand, possibly failing a subject or two, but completely unconcerned, because the joy of discovery outweighs every missed hour of sleep.

What keeps me going is a mix of curiosity, passion, and sheer stubborn love for learning. Every dataset I analyze, every equation I solve, and every star or galaxy I study reminds me how vast and mysterious the universe is. Astronomy and cosmology are not just fields of study for me; they are a playground where I challenge myself, learn my limits, and occasionally laugh at the absurdity of trying to comprehend infinite space.

Professional Experience

Intern

Indian Institute of Astrophysics (IIA)

May 2025 - November 2025 (Remote)

I am working on understanding the stellar populations in the western outskirts of the Small Magellanic Cloud (SMC), a nearby dwarf galaxy interacting with both the Milky Way and the Large Magellanic Cloud. My research is conducted under the guidance of Prof. Annapurni Subramaniam and Postdoc Scholar Dr. Sipra Hota.

We study a region centered on the intermediate-age SMC cluster Kron 3, observed in both Far-UV and Near-UV bands of UVIT/Astrosat. Photometry and cross-matching with Gaia allow us to identify multiple stellar populations, including SMC members and foreground Milky Way stars.

This work demonstrates how combining UV and optical data can effectively separate overlapping populations and provides insights into galaxy evolution, cluster interactions, and the structure of nearby galaxies.

Research Projects

Quasi-Normal Modes of Schwarzschild Black Holes with Dark Matter Halos

Ongoing

Supervisor: Dr. Suman Ghosh, Birla Institute of Technology, Mesra

Investigation of quasi-normal modes (QNMs) of Schwarzschild black holes with scalar perturbations, focusing on the influence of surrounding dark matter distributions on gravitational wave signatures. This research aims to understand how dark matter halos affect the ringdown phase of black hole mergers.

Methodology:

  • Numerical solution of perturbation equations
  • Analysis of QNM frequencies and damping times
  • Comparison with LIGO/Virgo observations
  • Computational modeling using Python and MATLAB
